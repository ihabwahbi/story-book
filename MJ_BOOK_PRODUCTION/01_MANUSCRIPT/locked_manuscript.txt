--- Page 1 ---
MJ and the Wobbly Days
A story about courage, difference, and living with MS
Written by MJ Donnellan

--- Page 2 ---
MJ loved to move.

--- Page 3 ---
She danced while breakfast cooked.
She spun across the kitchen floor in her socks.

--- Page 4 ---
She ran barefoot through the grass.
She twirled until the world went dizzy and fell into giggles.
Movement made MJ feel free.

--- Page 5 ---
Until one day…
something changed.

--- Page 6 ---
Her legs felt strange.
Heavy.
Wobbly.

--- Page 7 ---
Her hands dropped things she was sure she was holding.
And some mornings, MJ woke up tired before the day had even begun.

--- Page 8 ---
At first, nobody understood.
Not even MJ.
'Maybe you just need more sleep,' people said.
'Maybe you're doing too much.'

--- Page 9 ---
But MJ knew.
Something didn't feel right.

--- Page 10 ---
Then one day, after her legs shook and her eyes went blurry, MJ found herself in a big hospital room, holding her mum's hand so tightly her fingers hurt.

--- Page 11 ---
That was the day the doctors said words MJ had never heard before:
Multiple Sclerosis.
MS.

--- Page 12 ---
Her mum rubbed circles on her hand.
'What does that mean?' MJ whispered.

--- Page 13 ---
The doctor smiled kindly.
'It means the messages from your brain to your body sometimes get a little mixed up.'

--- Page 14 ---
MJ blinked.
'Like bad phone reception?'
The doctor laughed softly.
'A little bit like that.'

--- Page 15 ---
Sometimes MS made walking harder.
Sometimes it made MJ tired.
Sometimes sore.
Sometimes shaky.
And sometimes scared.

--- Page 16 ---
'Will I always be different?' MJ asked quietly.
Her mum squeezed her hand.
'Yes,' she said gently.
'But different does not mean less.'

--- Page 17 ---
That didn't make everything easier.
There were still hard days.
Days MJ fell over.
Days her legs wouldn't listen.
Days she cried because her body felt cross with her.

--- Page 18 ---
Days people said things that hurt.
'But you don't look sick.'

--- Page 19 ---
MJ wished they could see what she felt.
The aching in her legs.
The tiredness behind her smile.
The fear that came when she had to walk across a room by herself.

--- Page 20 ---
But little by little…
MJ learned something.
She was still MJ.
Still funny.
Still brave.
Still full of colour.
Still full of dreams.

--- Page 21 ---
On wobbly days, she moved slower.
On tired days, she rested.
On hard days, she asked for help.

--- Page 22 ---
And one afternoon, while painting swirls of bright orange across a canvas, her little cousin asked:
'What are you making?'
MJ smiled.
'My messy storm.'

--- Page 23 ---
Her cousin frowned.
'It looks sunny too.'
MJ grinned.
'It can be both.'

--- Page 24 ---
That made her cousin think very hard.
Then he smiled.
'Oh.'
And maybe life could be like that too.

--- Page 25 ---
Hard and beautiful.
Scary and brave.
Different and wonderful.

--- Page 26 ---
Soon MJ began sharing her story.
She told children that some differences are easy to see…
and some are hidden.

--- Page 27 ---
Some people use wheelchairs.
Some wear hearing aids.
Some need medicine.
Some need extra rest.
And everyone deserves kindness.

--- Page 28 ---
One little girl raised her hand.
'So being different isn't bad?'
MJ smiled.
'No.'

--- Page 29 ---
She looked around the room.
'Different just means we all have different stories.'
A quiet voice whispered from the back:
'That makes the world more interesting.'

--- Page 30 ---
MJ felt tears sting her eyes.
Because once, she thought being different meant something was wrong with her.
But maybe…
it just meant her story looked different.

--- Page 31 ---
That night, MJ stood at her window looking at the stars.
Her legs hurt.
Her body was tired.
But her heart felt full.

--- Page 32 ---
Because even on the wobbly days…
she was still learning.
Still growing.
Still shining.
And being different?
That was never something to hide.
That was something to honour.
